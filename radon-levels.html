Creating a Model for Radon Levels in Houses

Overview

Radon is a carcinogenic naturally ocurring gas which is estimated to cause thousands of lung cancer deaths every year in the United States.

In this project I modelled 85 counties in Minnesota using a heirarchal model.

Assumptions

This model requires us to make a couple key assumptions

  1. Counties come from some underlying distribution based on some hyper paramaters.
  2. Houses vary within a specific county dependant on that county.

Is this reasonable?

We are asserting that houses are part of some distribution within a specific county (in this case based on soil radon levels) and that counties themselves are part of some distribution.

The first assumption seems obviously reasonable, and the second makes a lot of sense when you consider the counties would be subject to similar legislation and building codes being in Minnesota and the United States as a whole.

The Predictors

  • County Level: Uranium measurement of the soil.
  • House level : Whether or not the measurement was taken in the basement.

Training

Just like great athletes , variational autoencoders need lots of training.

We will do this by trying to make sure when we deconstruct and reconstruct an image we get close to the same thing back.

Mathematically we are maximizing a lower bound estimate of the loss which is outlined in the paper this is based on.

Implementing this in a scalable way:

                
  def vae_lower_bound(gen_params, rec_params, data, rs):
    # We use a simple Monte Carlo estimate of the KL
    # divergence from the prior.
    q_means, q_log_stds = encoder(data , rec_params)
    latents = sample_diag_gaussian(q_means , q_log_stds,npr.RandomState(0))
    log_q_z = diag_gaussian_log_density(latents,q_means,q_log_stds)
    log_prior_z = log_prior(latents)
    log_p_x_given_z = log_likelihood(gen_params, data, latents)
    joint = log_prior_z + log_p_x_given_z
    elbo_estimate = np.mean(joint - log_q_z)
    return elbo_estimate
